How Can the City of Toronto Optimize Space to Create Effiencies?

To have more funds to help with the affordability crisis in Toronto, we need to create more efficiencies throughout the urban fabric. Creating areas with more than one function is a great way to do this. For example, when building parks, include a flood reservoir. This flood control mechanism can be a great addition to a park. In a vegetated trial, a flood reservoir acting like a river can give the park a feel of authenticity, as well as provide flood protection for the city. This is one way in how we can create efficiencies throughout Torontoâ€™s landmass.

Another great example of this would be to have multi-purpose parking lots. For example, the City can organize the commercial sector to have two major events happening at different times of the day. A parking lot can be used for an office complex during the day. At night, when most of the employees have gone home, the parking lot can then be used to hold nightclub goers. Through this, we are best optimizing the use of limited space that we have.

With this, we can cut the amount of land we use and preserve it for later development. The City of Toronto can also save scare money that can used to contribute to other things that desperately need to be corrected, such as the affordability crisis.
